President Obama will appoint Georgetown University Law Center visiting professor John Podesta (LAW ’76) to his administration.
As reported in the New York Times, Podesta will soon begin a one year term as counselor to President Obama after helping to lead the president’s transition to the Oval Office in 2008. Podesta also served in the first Clinton administration as an assistant to the president and deputy chief of staff.
Podesta has been a summer faculty member at the Law Center since 1997. He began teaching at the Law Center as a visiting professor in 1995.
As founder and former head of the Center for American Progress, a D.C. based public policy think tank, Podesta promoted a progressive agenda focused on health care, immigration, and energy issues.
Regarded as one of the Democratic Party’s most experienced strategists, Podesta will help offset the loss of a number of close advisers to Obama in coming months including Pete Rouse, the president’s counselor and a leading Congressional expert.
According to The New York Times, Podesta will largely assist McDonough on issues related to health care law, administration organization and executive action.
Hoya Staff Writer

<p>
	Mason Inn and George bar owner Fritz Brogan (COL ’07, LAW ’10) will not operate the Healey Family Student Center pub after failing to reach a satisfactory financial agreement with the university.</p>




<p>
	Brogan’s company, Hilltop Hospitality, signed a letter of intent in April, emerging as the university’s best candidate for the pub over 18 other vendors, including <span data-scayt_word="Aramark" data-scaytid="1">Aramark</span> Higher Education, which manages <span data-scayt_word="O’Donovan" data-scaytid="2">O’Donovan</span> Hall.</p>




<p>
	“The university and my company were unable to reach a financial agreement that satisfied our substantial investment of financial and other resources into the project,” Brogan wrote in an email to The Hoya.</p>




<p>
	The third-generation alumnus had promised to solicit student feedback on features of the pub, which was to accept payment by <span data-scayt_word="GOCard" data-scaytid="3">GOCard</span> in addition to cash and credit and offer a walk-up style dining service under the proposed name of Stonewall. The pub was to be open to students 18 years and older, with wristbands or hand stamps used to distinguish students over 21. A planning forum was held in August to discuss these possibilities, and another was scheduled for the beginning of the school year but never came to fruition.</p>




<p>
	“Mr. Brogan informed us several weeks ago that this model is not one he wants to pursue after all,” Associate Vice President and Chief Business Officer Deborah <span data-scayt_word="Morey" data-scaytid="6">Morey</span> said. “We are grateful for the time and energy that he put into these discussions. The university has benefited from them, and they will help inform our conversations with other vendors going forward.”</p><div class='code-block code-block-1' style='margin: 8px 0; clear: both;'>
<div style="padding: 10px; background: #f3f3f3; font-size: 12px; margin-bottom:20px;">Ad:<div style="padding:5px;"><a target="_blank" href="https://shareasale.com/r.cfm?b=1846437&amp;u=2942053&amp;m=113278&amp;urllink=&amp;afftrack="><img src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%200%200'%3E%3C/svg%3E" border="0" alt="The wear-wnywhere go any-where-tee" data-lazy-src="https://static.shareasale.com/image/113278/mia-banner-ad-4.jpg" /><noscript><img  border="0" alt="The wear-wnywhere go any-where-tee" data-src="https://static.shareasale.com/image/113278/mia-banner-ad-4.jpg" class="lazyload" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAAAAACH5BAEKAAEALAAAAAABAAEAAAICTAEAOw==" /><noscript><img src="https://static.shareasale.com/image/113278/mia-banner-ad-4.jpg" border="0" alt="The wear-wnywhere go any-where-tee" /></noscript></noscript></a></div></div></div>





<p>
	Associate Vice President for Auxiliary Services Joelle <span data-scayt_word="Wiese" data-scaytid="7">Wiese</span> has convened a panel of students to explore other vendors that has met twice thus far, according to Georgetown University Student Association President Nate <span data-scayt_word="Tisa" data-scaytid="8">Tisa</span> (<span data-scayt_word="SFS" data-scaytid="11">SFS</span> ’14). The informal group, composed of <span data-scayt_word="GUSA" data-scaytid="12">GUSA</span> members and other students who had previously expressed interest in the pub, are looking to find a vendor that will maintain the predetermined ambience.</p>




<p>
	Designs for the 3,000-square-foot indoor space and outdoor patio have already been presented to students at planning forums.</p>




<p>
	A timeline for selection of a new vendor has yet to be determined. The student center that will house the pub is currently set to open in fall 2014.</p>




<p>
	“Once there’s more progress, and we’re further along in the process of identifying potential vendors, I think the goal is to have a broader discussion, look at options and then have another forum or two on looking at the pub process,” <span data-scayt_word="Tisa" data-scaytid="13">Tisa</span> said. “We should do that again with the new vendor.”</p>




<p>
	In his email to The Hoya, Brogan expressed gratitude for Tisa’s involvement with the pub. He also spoke of his view on the project.
</p>
